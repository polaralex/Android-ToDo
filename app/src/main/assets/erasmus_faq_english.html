Frequently Asked Questions:

Can I join the Erasmus programme?

Yes. If you are an undergraduate, postgraduate or PhD of our University, or even if you are finishing your studies soon, you have the right to submit an application.

For how long can I stay abroad?

From 3 to 6 months, if you are interested in studies, and from 2 to 4 months if you are interested in an internship.

Will I receive a grant?

You will get a grant, relative to your type of Erasmus, the length and the receiving country, after your application has been accepted.

When should I submit the application?

Your application must be submitted before the deadline of the proclamation that is published once a year.

Is the process of the application difficult?

No. But you need to be organized and spend some time to get ready. The best way to begin is to carefully study all the details about the Erasmus programme and the application on our website, and to also study the details of the Proclamation.

Should I speak the language of the receiving country?

Yes, if it's one of the countries with widely spoken languages (e.g. UK, France, Germany, Italy and Spain - with a language level of at least B2). On the rest of the countries, you need at least a B2 level in English.

Are the courses that I attend in the receiving University being recognized?

Yes, if you have done all of the process before your application correctly.

How can I keep up with important news and announcements?

Like our Facebook page, where all news concerning the Erasmus programme are posted.
